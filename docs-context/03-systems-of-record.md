This file describes the three operational platforms Helm reads from — AppFolio, Buildertrend, and Podio — plus the two downstream accounting consumers (BNA, QuickBooks → Sage) that depend on the data those platforms produce. For where each data element is *born* and how it must *propagate*, see `05-data-sync-map.md`. For the phase model referenced throughout (PH-1..PH-Z), see `02-property-lifecycle.md`. For the audit rules that fire when these systems disagree, see `06-audit-rules.md`.

## 1. The architecture in one paragraph

Dominion runs separate platforms for separate business functions, and each platform acts as an **independent sub-ledger**. Nothing reconciles them automatically today — the accounting team uses manual and systematic checkpoints daily to keep records aligned. On top of that, operational staff **double-key status updates into Podio alongside whichever system actually runs the work** (Buildertrend during construction, AppFolio during turnover/leasing). Helm exists to derive a single cross-platform picture (the property registry and phase "Hub" snapshot), flag disagreements (the D-rule audit), and — per Jack — eventually absorb Podio's role entirely. Which platform is the *source of truth* is not global but moves with the lifecycle — Buildertrend during renovation (Podio Renovation mirrors it), AppFolio after the DP→DM turnover (Podio then a compliance supplement) — per Jack, decision 18 (2026-07-12); see §6.

```
                 ┌────────────┐   ┌──────────────┐   ┌─────────┐
   operational → │  AppFolio  │   │ Buildertrend │   │  Podio  │ ← legacy middleware,
   systems       │ (DM/rental)│   │ (DP/constr.) │   │ (both)  │   being absorbed
                 └─────┬──────┘   └──────┬───────┘   └────┬────┘
                       └────────────┬────┴─────────┬──────┘
                                    ▼              ▼
                              ┌──────────┐   ┌───────────────────────┐
   downstream  →              │   HELM   │   │ Accounting consumers: │
   consumers                  │ (derive, │   │ BNA (fixed assets)    │
                              │  audit)  │   │ QuickBooks → Sage '26 │
                              └──────────┘   └───────────────────────┘
```

Scope note: this covers the DM/DP rental-and-construction side only. DFS (lending) runs its own systems and is out of Helm scope.

## 2. AppFolio — DM's property management system

**Owner-operator:** Dominion Management (DM). Every rental entity and individual asset owner is set up as an active owner account inside AppFolio.

**What lives there**

- Tenant ledgers and tenant accounts receivable (AR)
- Leases and lease dates; rent rolls; security deposit escrow tracking
- Rental applications and approvals
- Move-ins / move-outs, occupancy status
- Operational maintenance and repair activity
- Rental distributions to owner accounts (feeds the monthly NOI reconciliation DM performs before distributing to owner entities)

**Who keys it in, and when (lifecycle phase)**

- **Stephanie Derry's team (DM Leasing & Compliance)** drives the revenue side: applications, approvals, leases, rent rolls, deposits — primarily PH-5 (Marketing/Vacancy) through PH-8 (Active rental), and again at PH-9 (Notice).
- **Craig Cuocci's team (DM Maintenance & Inspections)** drives maintenance/turnover activity visible in AppFolio — PH-8 through PH-10.
- During turnover & leasing, this same status information is **double-keyed into Podio** (the Occupancy workspace's Vacant Units and Sched Vacants apps; rent fields on Lead Certs & Water Bills items) — see §4 and `05-data-sync-map.md`.

**Downstream consumers**

- DM's monthly property-level reconciliation and NOI distribution to owner entities (booked in QuickBooks today, Sage in 2026).
- Staff accountants use AppFolio activity to classify turnover bills as DM operating expense vs. DP capitalized cost.
- Helm derives leasing-side phase evidence (applications, move-ins). **Correction (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11):** the "matched leases" layer behind the Avg Lease Time KPI joins AppFolio `occupancy.move_in_date` to Podio **Vacant Unit** records (`days = move_in_date − COALESCE(vacant_unit.marketing_start_date, intake_date)`), NOT to Lead Certs & Water Bills rent records — the earlier draft mislabeled the source app. See `04-cycle-time-model.md` for the exact KPI definition (all-time, 0–365d outlier filter, per-(occupancy,tenant) so co-tenants double-count).

## 3. Buildertrend — DP's construction job-costing system

**Owner-operator:** Dominion Properties (DP), the general contractor for all sister entities.

**What lives there**

- **Every project, substantial rehab, and turnover is an independent job.**
- Purchase orders (POs), bills, bids, and owner invoices per job.
- Subcontractor field invoices, which **PMs approve** as operational validation before the Friday Net-5 ACH labor runs.
- Job logs that staff accountants **cross-reference against material supplier invoices** before payment (DP buys most materials directly to eliminate markup; subs supply only trade-standard materials like wire or air handlers).

**Who keys it in, and when (lifecycle phase)**

- **Project Managers** (reporting to James Stewart, Director of Field Operations) manage job sites and keep Buildertrend current — primarily PH-2 (post-closing handoff) through PH-3 (Renovation), and PH-10 (Vacancy/Turn) when DP runs a turnover with its 6 in-house contractors.
- During construction, project statuses are **double-keyed into Podio** as well — see §4.

**Downstream consumers**

- **BNA (Bloomberg Tax Fixed Assets):** initial-renovation costs are fully capitalized; when a job completes and the property is turned over to DM for leasing, it is added to BNA for depreciation.
- **QuickBooks → Sage:** job bills feed the AP disbursement calendar (suppliers within 7 days; labor Net-5 Friday ACH) and DP's month-end overhead allocation, which is apportioned by hard cost per property and capitalized at the property-LLC level.
- Helm derives construction-side phase evidence (jobs, POs, bills) and audit rule D1 ("Closing complete but no Buildertrend job"). The **"Handed Off - Rental" string is a value of the Podio Renovation app's Phase field, not a Buildertrend value** (per Podio API tour 2026-07-09, see §4). **Correction (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11, superseding the earlier note that this "resolved the driver's source system"):** in the code "Handed Off - Rental" does NOT drive the live phase at all. `derivePhase()` (`apps/backend/src/modules/phase/phase.service.ts`) never matches it — a Handed-Off property fails the `/complete/i` test and falls into the **PH-3 catch-all** unless an AppFolio unit/occupancy rule fires first. The string is honored only by (a) the property-detail retrospective **journey/history** deriver (`deriveHistoryFromDates`, regex `/^Handed Off/i` → a PH-4 journey card) and (b) D9's turnover-exclusion list. So it drives the retrospective journey, never the current-phase number. See the full PH-3/PH-4 correction in `02-property-lifecycle.md`. **Intent (per Jack, decision 18 (2026-07-12)):** during PH-3, **Buildertrend is the source of construction truth and the Podio Renovation Phase field is a MIRROR of BT progress** — so on a construction-state disagreement BT's `schedule_status` wins and a divergent Podio Renovation.phase is the stale mirror (discrepancy direction for common-data row 8 / candidate rule D3). Turnover is Jack's PH-3→PH-4 handoff — a completed BT job + Podio "Handed Off - Rental" + an AppFolio asset created under the owner. That is Jack's intent; the code gap above is unchanged — `derivePhase()` still can't see "Handed Off - Rental", so Helm doesn't yet register the handoff at the current-phase layer. See §6 for the phase-scoped authority model.

## 6. Source-of-truth authority — segment custodian (decided) vs. per-field winner (deferred)

**Segment-level source of truth is now DECIDED: authority moves with the lifecycle — per Jack, decision 18 (2026-07-12).** The property lives in the Podio Renovation app for its whole reno life, but the *custodian* of truth changes by lifecycle segment:

- **PH-3 (Renovation) → Buildertrend is the source; Podio Renovation MIRRORS it.** BT is the tool the construction department actually works in, so its progress data is authoritative and the Podio Renovation Phase field is a mirror. On a construction-state disagreement, **BT `schedule_status` wins and a divergent Podio Renovation.phase is a stale mirror** — this sets the discrepancy direction for common-data row 8 / candidate rule D3 (see `10-common-data-map.md`).
- **Turnover (PH-3→PH-4) is a 3-part synchronized handoff.** A **property turnover email is sent by DP to DM** and, as one event, (a) the Buildertrend job is complete, (b) the Podio Renovation Phase becomes **"Handed Off - Rental"**, and (c) the property is **added to AppFolio as an asset under the appropriate owner**. The AppFolio-asset-creation is the concrete PH-4 event; any leg present without the others is a discrepancy (see `05-data-sync-map.md` and the turnover-triad candidate rules in `10-common-data-map.md`).
- **PH-4+ (post-handoff) → AppFolio is primary for day-to-day management; Podio is a compliance supplement only.** Podio carries the compliance records AppFolio can't track (D17/D18 licensing & lead-paint on the Lead Certs & Water Bills app). This residual Podio role is **intended, not drift** — it is precisely why compliance data legitimately stays in Podio after AppFolio becomes the primary source.

**Intent vs. code (code gap unchanged, per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11).** Decision 18 is Jack's *intent*. **Scheduler & ingestion cadence (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11).** There is **no in-repo cron.** Ingestion is passive: an external cron must POST `/scheduler/tick` every minute; jobs fire the incremental pipeline at **02:00 UTC** and the phase snapshot at **03:00 UTC** (catch-up-if-missed). If the external cron is lost on a redeploy, ingestion **silently stops**. Because the combined Podio crawl (1–4h) + BT bids scrape (~6h) can run most of a working day, the "02:00 incremental" often isn't complete until well into the next day — which reinforces the day-lag assumption above.

**All ingestion is read-only inbound (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11).** Helm writes only to its own Postgres — there is **no write-back** to AppFolio, Podio, or Buildertrend (Podio POSTs are only OAuth + the read-query filter endpoint; AppFolio is report reads only; BT only navigates/downloads). This is the hard prerequisite behind the Podio-retirement roadmap (`05-data-sync-map.md` §5): the write path Stages 2–3 assume **does not exist yet**, and remediation of every audit finding is manual by design (the rules instruct a human).

**Practical consequence for conflict handling:** because AppFolio is live-ish and Buildertrend is day-delayed, some cross-platform "discrepancies" will be pure timing skew. **Proposal:** the audit engine (see `06-audit-rules.md`) could suppress or age-gate findings younger than the Buildertrend refresh interval before flagging them as real. **Caveat (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11):** age-gating is **not possible today without new infrastructure** — audit findings are NOT persisted (every `/audit/discrepancies` call re-runs all 9 rules live; `detected_at` = request time; no dismiss/snooze/resolve/age-gating). So this stays a proposal that first requires building finding persistence. Trade-off if built: age-gating also hides *real* findings for up to a day — likely acceptable given Jack's stated tolerance for delayed Buildertrend data, but it is a deliberate latency-for-noise trade, not a free win.
