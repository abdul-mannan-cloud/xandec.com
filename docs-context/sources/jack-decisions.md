# Jack's Answers — Helm Discussion (2026-07-06)

These are direct decisions from Jack BeVier. They are AUTHORITATIVE and override inference.

1. **Scope confirmed**: Helm covers DM & DP — the rental & construction side. (Not DFS lending.)

2. **Cycle-time philosophy — every day matters, and matters the same.** Jack: "The opportunity cost of every day that we own a property that isn't rented is 1 day of rent, or 1 day of carry + opportunity cost of our capital. Every day matters and matters the same." Implication: there is no hierarchy of "important" phase clocks. The master metric is days-not-rented (carry days) per property across the whole lifecycle; per-phase clocks exist to locate WHERE the days accumulate, not to rank which delays matter.

3. **The duplicate entry, concretely**: While a property is in construction, staff must update BOTH Podio and Buildertrend with project statuses. While a property is in turnover & leasing, staff must update BOTH Podio and AppFolio with status updates. The double-keyed data is status updates into Podio alongside the operational system.

Design consequence: treat Podio as a legacy source being absorbed — new Helm features should not deepen Podio dependency, and Helm-native records should be able to take over the roles of the Podio workspaces (acquisitions/closing, vacant units, rental billing, compliance & licensing).

5. **Integration reality**: AppFolio — API access in hand. Buildertrend — manual downloads of daily data, but the downloads can be triggered by an automated workflow (slightly delayed, acceptable; same-day freshness is not critical). Podio — not stated by Jack (Podio has a full public API; current pull mechanism [confirm]).

## Acquisition-phase detail (added 2026-07-09)

10. **Seller-type fork on pre-closing work:**

    - **Private-individual sellers:** utilities are NOT put in Dominion's name and the property is NOT trashed out until AFTER closing, to avoid the drama of accidentally removing items with sentimental value to the seller.

18. **Source-of-truth moves with the lifecycle — the renovation→turnover→leasing handoff** (2026-07-12, authoritative). Jack's exact model:

    - When a property is ready to be leased, a **property turnover email is sent by Dominion Properties (DP) to Dominion Management (DM)**, turning the property over to DM for leasing. At that point: (a) the property is a **completed job in Buildertrend**; (b) in Podio the Renovation status is updated to **"Handed Off - Rental"**; (c) the property is **added to AppFolio as an asset under the appropriate owner.**
      - **Example-confirmed (2026-07-12):** a real DP→DM completion email is saved at `sources/example-turnover-email-dp-to-dm.md` ("103 S. Collins Turnover to DM"). 20. **The disposition (sale) lifecycle — the PH-X arm; Renovation.Phase ↔ Sale Closings move together** (2026-07-12, authoritative). Jack: "When a property is going to be sold, the Phase in the Renovation app will be On Market. Once we accept a contract on the property, the Phase is updated to Under Contract. You should expect to see an item created in Sale Closings for each property that we have Under Contract. The property will stay in that Phase until the property is Sold, in which case the phase in Renovation is updated to Sold and the Sale Closing app Status is updated to Sold."
    - **The disposition state machine:** Renovation.Phase **On Market** (listed for sale) → **Under Contract** (offer accepted) → **Sold** (closed). A **Sale Closings item is created when the property goes Under Contract** (one per under-contract property), and at close **both** Renovation.Phase and Sale Closings.Status are set to **Sold**.
    - **This is a synchronized PAIR** (the disposition analogue of the turnover triad): Renovation.Phase and Sale Closings.Status track each other. Expected invariants: Renovation "Under Contract" ⇔ a Sale Closings item exists (Status Under Contract); Renovation "Sold" ⇔ Sale Closings.Status "Sold".
    - **New state-consistency candidates (feed `10-common-data-map.md` §4):** (a) Renovation.Phase = "Under Contract" but NO Sale Closings item (sale record never created); (b) Sale Closings "Under Contract" but Renovation.Phase ≠ "Under Contract"; (c) Renovation.Phase = "Sold" but Sale Closings.Status ≠ "Sold" (or vice versa) — the two Sold flags out of sync; (d) Sale Closings.Status = "Sold" but AppFolio still `is_active` = the existing candidate **D21** ("sale-disposed in Podio but still active in AppFolio") — now confirmed as the disposition end-state check.
    - **This is the PH-X (disposition) arm — distinct from PH-Z (write-off).** On Market and Under Contract are disposition-IN-PROGRESS states; PH-X (Sold) is terminal. The Renovation app therefore spans the ENTIRE property life — acquisition prep → construction → turnover → disposition (On Market / Under Contract / Sold) — confirming the breadth of its 43-value Phase vocabulary.
    - **CODE GAP:** live `derivePhase()` does NOT read Renovation.Phase values "On Market" / "Under Contract" / "Sold" — none are in the write-off list and none match `/complete/i`, so they fall into the **PH-3 catch-all** (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11). Helm's PH-X fires only on **compliance `lifecycle_stage='disposed'` AND `is_active=false`** (lifecycle_stage sourced from the Podio compliance boards' property-status, not from Renovation or Sale Closings). So a property that is On Market / Under Contract / Sold in Renovation is largely **invisible to Helm's live disposition detection** unless the separate compliance property-status is also set to disposed and AppFolio is deactivated. Reading Renovation.Phase (On Market/Under Contract/Sold) and/or Sale Closings.Status is net-new work to make dispositions visible.
