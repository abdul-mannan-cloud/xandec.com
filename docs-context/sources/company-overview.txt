1. Systems Architecture & Internal Controls
Review this section first to understand the layout of our files, sub-ledgers, and mandatory monthly reconciliation cycles.
The portfolio uses separate software platforms for different business functions. Because these platforms act as independent sub-ledgers, the accounting team must use manual and systematic checkpoints daily to keep records aligned.
* Property Management / Tenant Ledgers: AppFolio. Every rental entity and individual asset owner is set up as an active owner account within the system to track rental distributions, tenant accounts receivable, and operational maintenance.
* Construction Job Costing / Project Management: Buildertrend. Every project, substantial rehab, and turnover is tracked as an independent job.
* Fixed Assets / Tax Depreciation: Bloomberg Tax Fixed Assets (BNA). Note: The internal accounting team maintains and logs all depreciation entries in BNA directly, rather than outsourcing them to our CPA.
* Operational Middleware (The Gap-Filler): Podio. Podio handles the operational workflows and compliance records that AppFolio and Buildertrend do not capture. Accountants will look into specific Podio workspaces for:
   * Acquisitions & Titles Workspace: Settlement statements (ALTA/HUD-1s) used by the Controller to establish cost basis in BNA.
   * Compliance & Licensing Workspace: Active MDE Lead Paint Certificates and rental licenses used by Staff Accountants to validate municipal invoice approvals.
The Accounting Ledger Environment
* Current State: QuickBooks Desktop. Every single entity that files a tax return operates out of its own independent QuickBooks Desktop company file. Because files are completely isolated, all intercompany transfers and Due To/Due From balances must be completely cleared and zeroed out monthly via bank transfer.
* Target State (2026 Migration): The team is currently migrating to Sage. Sage will centralize the portfolio into a single database with a unified Chart of Accounts and automated intercompany balancing.
* Capital Accounts / Preferential Returns: Calculated and booked exactly once per year (1x/yr). The Controller tracks the 8% accrued preferred returns on a master offline spreadsheet, allocates profits up to that threshold, and applies excess percentages before pushing the annual adjusting journals to the ledgers.
* External Debt Payments: Paid directly out of individual Parent Entity or Property LLC bank accounts shortly after receiving the net monthly NOI checks from DM. Debt service does not clear out of the main DM or DP operational accounts.

3. Executive Summary & Core Mission
Founded in 2002 by Fred Lewis, the portfolio consists of an integrated real estate business specializing in property management, development, construction, and rental operations primarily within Maryland.
The corporate structure uses distinct legal entities to isolate operational and construction liabilities, protect single-asset real estate investments, and manage tax and insurance requirements.
The Universal Ownership Blueprint
With the exception of Emerson Point LLC, every entity in this portfolio shares the exact same equity and economic structure:
* Fred Lewis: 1% Class A Voting Member (Holds 100% of decision-making authority)
* The Lewis Family Trust: 79% Class A Non-Voting Member
* Jack BeVier: 20% Class B Non-Voting Member
Critical Accounting Directive: The economic interest of all ownership classes is identical. All capital accounts accrue an 8% preferred return first. Excess profits are then apportioned strictly based on ownership percentage (79% / 20% / 1%), regardless of voting status.

5. Intercompany & Operational Financial Workflows
This section outlines the rules governing how money, fees, and allocations move between the operational companies and the asset holders.
A. Property Management Revenue & Cash Flows (DM)
* The Relationship: Governed by formal, market-rate, arm's-length Property Management Agreements (PMAs) between DM and each property-owning entity.
* The Cash Flow Sequence:
   1. All tenant rental income across the entire 1,000-unit portfolio is deposited directly into DM's accounts.
   2. DM pays all day-to-day property-level operating expenses (repairs, maintenance, turn costs) out of its centralized operational accounts.
   3. Monthly, DM reconciles property-level performance and distributes Net Operating Income (NOI)—net of management fees and operating expenses—directly to the owners.
* Banking Access Rules:
   * Each Rental Parent Entity maintains its own funded bank account to receive monthly NOI distributions and directly pay its own Property Taxes and Insurance bills.
   * Subsidiary Title-Holding LLCs do NOT have bank accounts. Their cash flows clear completely through DM, with final NOI distributed to the Parent Entity bank accounts.
   * Woodson and Park Heights LLCs receive their monthly NOI distributions directly from DM into their respective property-level bank accounts.
B. Construction Management & Overhead Allocation (DP)
DP charges an overhead allocation to recover corporate labor and administrative costs for all development, rehabs, and turnovers.
* The Allocation Method: A master internal spreadsheet tracks DP's monthly corporate overhead. At month-end, the overhead is apportioned based on the amount spent in hard costs on each specific property.
* The Property Ownership LLC Ledger: Because the underlying TICs do not maintain separate sets of books, the allocated overhead is billed and applied directly to the Property Ownership LLC level (e.g., 901-903 Woodson Ave LLC or 6001 Park Heights Ave LLC).
* The Accounting Treatment: This overhead allocation MUST be capitalized by the property ownership LLC (added to Construction in Progress / asset basis), rather than expensed.
C. The Property Lifecycle & Internal Contractor Labor
* Initial Renovations: Performed by DP before a property is placed in service. All labor and material costs are tracked in Buildertrend and fully capitalized. Once complete, the property is turned over to DM for leasing and added to the BNA depreciation software.
* Turnover Work: When a property goes vacant, DP manages the turnover using 6 in-house contractors to keep labor costs down and limit carrier costs. This turnover labor and material cost is typically routed as an operating repair/maintenance expense under DM's management ledger rather than capitalized.
6. Procurement, Accounts Payable, & Cash Disbursements
To maintain consistent relationships with crews and vendors, the accounting team operates on a rapid cash disbursement calendar. Manual checks are being phased out in favor of 100% ACH enrollment.
* Material Procurement Control: DP acts as a General Contractor, subcontracting labor but purchasing its own materials directly from suppliers to eliminate markup. Staff Accountants cross-reference material supplier invoices with Buildertrend job logs to ensure materials match the authorized scope before payment. There are strict operational safeguards in place to validate that the correct materials are purchased and applied to the correct Dominion jobs.
* Subcontractor Material Nuances: Subcontractors only supply materials when it is standard and cost-effective (e.g., electricians buy their own wire, HVAC companies buy their own air handlers). Otherwise, DP buys the materials to control costs.
The AP Disbursement Calendar
* Material Suppliers: Paid within 7 days of invoice receipt via rolling weekly payments, regardless of supplier credit lines.
* Labor & Subcontractors: Paid on a strict Net 5 basis. Invoices submitted by Monday morning at 9:00 AM are paid that same Friday afternoon via ACH.
