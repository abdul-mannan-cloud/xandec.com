# 06 — Audit Rules: The D-Rule Catalog

This file documents the cross-platform audit rules ("D-rules") that Helm fires today, the business meaning behind each one, who should own resolution, and which system gets corrected. This resolves the earlier [confirm]: the catalog is nine, not "roughly D1–D20," and the missing numbers are free for the candidate rules in §4 and in `10-common-data-map.md` to claim.

**These counts are a point-in-time query result, not a stored backlog.** Findings are not persisted — every `/audit/discrepancies` call re-runs all nine rules' SQL live and returns whatever is true at that instant; `detected_at` is the request timestamp, not when a discrepancy first appeared. The "423 findings" figures above are a live snapshot, not a work queue that ages. See §3.

### D1 — HIGH — "Closing complete but no Buildertrend job" (3 findings)

- **Lifecycle meaning:** Property has reached PH-2 (closing complete, handoff to construction) but PH-3 (renovation) has no operational anchor — no Buildertrend job exists. Per company practice, every project, substantial rehab, and turnover is tracked as an independent Buildertrend job, and initial renovations must be fully capitalized from Buildertrend cost data.
- **Why HIGH (strawman):** The carry clock started at closing. Under Jack's uniform day-cost philosophy (see 04-cycle-time-model.md), every day between closing and rent is a day of carry — a missing job means renovation likely hasn't been mobilized, and nobody's clock is running on it. Secondary risk: without a Buildertrend job there is no job-costing container, which breaks the capitalization basis that accounting builds from Buildertrend into BNA.
- **Owner (strawman):** Victoria Robinson (Transaction Coordinator) confirms the closing record is real and complete; James Stewart (Director of Field Operations) or the assigned PM creates the job. [confirm]
- **Playbook:** Buildertrend gets the fix — create the job and scope. If the property legitimately needs no renovation (bought rent-ready), the correct fix is instead a phase-model bypass to PH-4. [confirm: does a "no-reno acquisition" path exist, and should D1 exempt it?]
- **Code (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11):** fires when `podio_closing.status ILIKE '%closed%'`, the closing is within the last 2 years, and `property.is_active`, but **no** `bt_job` matches (by FK or by street/job-name). `d1-closing-no-bt-job.rule.ts:25-40`.

### D5 — MEDIUM — "Written off in Podio but still active in AppFolio" (not observed firing on tour)

- **Lifecycle meaning:** Podio's renovation board marks the property written off or parked — a PH-Z signal — while AppFolio still carries `property.is_active = true`. The two systems disagree on whether the property is a going concern: one has abandoned it, the other still counts it as live inventory. This is the code realization of candidate rule #1 in §4 ("sold/disposed in one system, active in another"), scoped narrowly to the Podio write-off/parked signal.
- **Why MEDIUM (code-assigned):** The app assigns MEDIUM. Rationale (strawman): a write-off AppFolio hasn't caught up to distorts active-inventory counts and can leak carry against a property nobody intends to operate, but it is a bookkeeping-state disagreement, not an active-tenant or cash problem.
- **Owner (strawman):** Accounting confirms the write-off/parked decision is real; whoever owns AppFolio property status deactivates it (or Podio is corrected if the write-off was a mistake). [confirm: who owns reconciling a Podio write-off against AppFolio active status — accounting or DM operations?]
- **Playbook:** Conform the two systems — deactivate in AppFolio if the write-off/parked decision stands, or clear the Podio write-off if it was applied in error.
- **Code (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11):** fires when `podio_renovation.phase IN ('Write Off','Parked','Write-Off','Write off') OR category = 'Parked'` while `property.is_active = true`. `d5-write-off-still-active.rule.ts:27-35`.

### D7 — MEDIUM — "AppFolio shows tenant moved in but Podio Vacant Unit not closed out" (6 findings)

- **Lifecycle meaning:** Property is really in PH-8 (active rental) per AppFolio, but the record in Podio's **Vacant Units** app (app 19820093, Occupancy workspace — per Podio API tour 2026-07-09) still shows it in the PH-5/PH-6/PH-7 pipeline. This is the leasing-side face of the double-entry burden Jack described: during turnover and leasing, staff must update both Podio and AppFolio, and here the Podio half was skipped.
- **Why MEDIUM (strawman):** No day-cost — rent is already flowing, so this is cheaper than any HIGH. But a stale open Vacant Unit pollutes the vacancy pipeline (Stephanie's leasing queue shows a unit that isn't vacant) and can trigger downstream errors in the Lead Certs & Water Bills app (the rent/billing record).
- **Owner (strawman):** Stephanie Derry (Director of DM Leasing & Compliance) resolves — closing out the Podio Vacant Unit is a leasing-records task, and her team drives the AppFolio revenue side. Craig Cuocci is notified, since the close-out is the administrative tail of his turn process. [confirm: D7 resolver — Stephanie (leasing close-out) or Craig (turn tail)?]
- **Playbook:** Podio gets the fix — AppFolio's move-in is authoritative (it is the tenant ledger of record; see 03-systems-of-record.md); close out the Vacant Unit record. Long-term, this entire rule disappears when Helm absorbs the Vacant Units app (see 05-data-sync-map.md).
- **Code (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11):** fires when AppFolio `occupancy.status = 'current'` (tenant moved in) but the matched `podio_vacant_unit.status NOT ILIKE '%contract%executed%'` (Podio still shows it open in the pipeline), with the intake date within move-in −180/+30 days, DISTINCT ON unit, `LIMIT 200`. App-assigned **MEDIUM**. `d7-…rule.ts:37-57`.

### D9 — HIGH — "Unit vacant more than 14 days with no turnover activity" (not observed firing on tour)

- **Lifecycle meaning:** A unit has been sitting vacant for more than 14 days and nothing is being done to turn it — no turnover, renovation, or handoff record exists to explain the gap. This is the pure carry-day case: vacant days accumulating with no work in flight. It closely resembles candidate rule #2 in §4 (vacancy with no marketing), though D9 keys on the absence of *turnover* records rather than the absence of an AppFolio listing.
- **Why HIGH (code-assigned):** The app assigns HIGH. Under Jack's uniform day-cost philosophy (see 04-cycle-time-model.md) every vacant day is a carry day; a vacancy past two weeks with no activity is avoidable leakage and directly actionable — start the turn or the marketing.
- **Owner (strawman):** Craig Cuocci (turns) if the unit needs work, Stephanie Derry (leasing) if it's turn-ready but unmarketed. [confirm: D9 resolver — Craig (turn not started) or Stephanie (marketing not started)?]
- **Playbook:** Start the missing activity — mobilize the turn or list the unit — so the vacancy has an owner and a clock.
- **Code (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11):** fires when `unit_status.code IN ('vacant_unrented','vacant_rented')` and `CURRENT_DATE − date_vacant > 14`, minus three `NOT EXISTS` turnover-activity exclusions. `d9-vacant-no-turnover.rule.ts:27-57`.

### D11 — LOW — "Owner LLC mismatch between Podio Vacant Units and Rental Billing" (200 findings)

- **Lifecycle meaning:** Two Podio apps disagree about which entity owns the property: **Vacant Units** (Occupancy workspace) vs. **Lead Certs & Water Bills** (Rentals Billing & Compliance workspace — the app the rule name calls "Rental Billing"); both carry hand-maintained Owner option lists, per Podio API tour 2026-07-09. Not a lifecycle-stage error at all — it is reference-data drift between two hand-keyed copies of the same fact.
- **Why LOW (strawman):** No operational day-cost and no tenant impact. But note the accounting stakes are not trivial: owner LLC determines which parent entity receives the monthly NOI distribution and which ledger the property lives on, and subsidiary title-holding LLCs vs. parents have different banking rules. Misallocation risk arguably argues for MEDIUM from accounting's perspective. [confirm: is LOW right, or does the Controller want these elevated?]
- **Owner (strawman):** Accounting resolves (Staff Accountants, escalating to the Controller) — owner LLC is an entity/ledger question, and they are the consumers of correct entity attribution. Victoria Robinson is consulted for the deed/settlement records that establish the true owner. [confirm: D11 resolver — accounting or Victoria?]
- **Playbook:** Determine the true owner from the authoritative chain — the deed/settlement statement from Victoria's acquisitions records, reflected in the AppFolio owner account — then correct whichever Podio app is wrong (usually both should be conformed to the deed). [confirm: which source does the team treat as authoritative for owner LLC — deed records or AppFolio owner accounts?]
- **Code (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11):** compares `podio_vacant_unit.type` (the "Owner" label) against `podio_property_compliance.owner_name`, case- and LLC-suffix-normalized, placeholder owners excluded, `LIMIT 200`. `d11-owner-mismatch.rule.ts:5-66`. Two corrections to the framing above: (1) the code's second source is the Podio **property-compliance** board (`podio_property_compliance`), which may not be the same "Lead Certs & Water Bills / Rental Billing" app the tour named for this rule — both are Podio boards, so the "two hand-keyed Podio copies" point stands regardless. (2) **This is the SIMPLIFIED, Podio-internal-only form:** it compares two Podio boards and does **not** compare either against the AppFolio owner account — the AppFolio-owner comparison is **explicitly deferred**, because there is no parent/child entity-hierarchy or alias table in the schema to resolve subsidiary title-holding LLCs against their parents. So the playbook's "reflected in the AppFolio owner account" step is aspirational, not what the rule checks today. The deferred AppFolio-owner version is tracked as the proposed **D12** in `10-common-data-map.md`, and the D12 number is genuinely free (never implemented).

### D14 — HIGH — "AppFolio application approved but no Podio Vacant Unit entry" (19 findings)

- **Lifecycle meaning:** Property should be in PH-6 (approved applicant, housing-program workflow) — the single biggest active pipeline bucket (61 properties) — but Podio has no record in the Vacant Units app (Occupancy workspace) to carry the workflow. Since Helm's phase derivation reads that app's Status vocabulary verbatim (per Podio API tour 2026-07-09), the missing record also deprives Helm of its PH-5/PH-6 signal for the property. The steps that live in Podio for this phase (per Stephanie's remit: Section 8/voucher processing, inspections, program paperwork) have no home. Note that Vacant Units records are normally *flow-created* (a GlobiFlow opens one when "vacant" is clicked in Sched Vacants — see `05-data-sync-map.md` §3.5), so a D14 finding likely means the flow chain never fired or the unit bypassed Sched Vacants — automation failure or path bypass, not simple forgetfulness.
- **Why HIGH (strawman):** The stall is silent — nothing fails in any system; the workflow record simply has no home, so the housing-program steps (inspections, voucher paperwork) can sit with no queue showing them to anyone. And the lost days are highly avoidable and actionable: an approved applicant is days from producing rent, so creating the missing record converts stalled days directly back into rent days. (Under Jack's uniform day-cost philosophy — see 04-cycle-time-model.md — no phase's day costs more than another's; the case for HIGH is silence plus actionability, not phase position.)
- **Owner (strawman):** Stephanie Derry. [confirm]
- **Playbook:** Podio gets the fix today — create the Vacant Unit entry so the program workflow can run. Note the design tension with Podio retirement: Jack wants Helm to eventually replace Podio, so the durable fix is a Helm-native leasing-workflow record, not deeper Podio dependence (see 05-data-sync-map.md).
- **Code (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11):** fires when an AppFolio `application.approved_at` is set (not denied/cancelled), the approval is >24h and <90d old, the unit is not occupied, and there is **no** active `podio_vacant_unit`. App-assigned **HIGH**. `d14-…rule.ts:38-63`.

### D20 — LOW — "Rent value disagreement between AppFolio and Podio" (135 findings)

- **Lifecycle meaning:** The rent Helm sees in AppFolio and the rent recorded in Podio disagree. Business framing (per Podio API tour 2026-07-09): the Podio side is the **Lead Certs & Water Bills** app, which carries three rent fields (Rent, Previous Rent, Current Rent) plus a Rent Increase Status workflow (Requested | Increase Received | Rent Confirmed Raised/In Compliance | Increase Denied | Increase Not Received). Like D11, this is drift between two copies of one fact — except where it isn't (see the caveats below).
- **Code (per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11):** D20 compares AppFolio **`unit.market_rent`** (the unit's *market* rent, **not** the signed-lease rent as the older draft assumed) against Podio **`podio_property_compliance.current_rent`**, flagging when `ABS(gap) > $50`, scoped to **Market-program units only** (not PH-8, and not any turnover state). `d20-rent-drift.rule.ts:33-51`. This resolves the "which of the three Podio rent fields" [confirm]: the field is **Current Rent**. Three code facts sharpen — and partly contradict — the framing above:
  - **No turnover gating exists.** Earlier drafts hoped D20 was (or should be) turnover-aware; the code has **none**. It survives Podio's turnover-clears-rent behavior only *incidentally*: when the GlobiFlow blanks the field, Podio's `fldMoney` returns NULL and the `current_rent IS NOT NULL` filter drops the row. If Podio ever wrote a literal `0` instead of clearing, D20 would false-fire on every turnover unit. (This supersedes the earlier "D20 is scoped to PH-8 so turnover units shouldn't hit it" reasoning from the GlobiFlow tour — the scope is Market-program, not PH-8, and nothing excludes turnover.)
  - **Multifamily cross-join defect.** The unit×compliance join fans out on multifamily buildings even though a `unit_id` column exists to key it — so a multifamily property can produce spurious/duplicated rent-drift rows, inflating the 135 count.
  - **No `podio_property_xref` fallback.** D11, D17, and D18 fall back to the xref link registry when the direct match misses; D20 does not, so it silently under-matches.
- **Why LOW (code-assigned):** The app assigns LOW. Rationale (strawman): the tenant is billed out of AppFolio (the tenant ledger of record), so cash collection is presumably unaffected; the damage is to any reporting or reconciliation that reads rent from Podio. [confirm: is anything downstream — owner reporting, NOI reconciliation, billing — actually driven off the Podio rent fields? If yes, LOW may understate it.]
- **Owner (strawman):** Accounting (Staff Accountants). [confirm]
- **Playbook:** Conform the Podio Current Rent to AppFolio where it is genuine drift — but two caveats mean "blindly conform to AppFolio" is wrong, and the code enforces **neither** guard: (1) the Rent Increase workflow means some Podio values are deliberately *intended-future* rent (an increase requested/received but not yet effective) — definitional disagreements, not drift. (2) The GlobiFlow "turnover = unset water billable information, rent, program & anniversary month" flow deliberately blanks Podio rent during turnover, so a blank rent on a turnover unit is expected state, not error. Because D20 has no turnover gating and no rent-increase-status check in code, both must be handled by whoever triages the findings (and by any one-time bulk reconciliation), not by the rule. [confirm: should D20 gain the turnover exclusion and Rent-Increase-Status suppression it currently lacks?]

## 3. The volume argument: drift, not errors

Read the counts, not just the rules. (Caveat, per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11: these counts are a **live query result, not a persisted backlog** — findings recompute on every request and are never stored, so the stock-vs-flow argument below is about the underlying *data* — real-world expiries, hand-keyed drift — not about an aging queue of findings you can track over time. You cannot see when a finding "first appeared"; `detected_at` is the request timestamp.)

- **D11 (200) + D20 (135) = 335 findings — 79% of everything the audit engine fires.** Both rules compare Podio apps — Vacant Units (Occupancy workspace) and Lead Certs & Water Bills (Rentals Billing & Compliance workspace) — against other records, i.e., exactly the double-keyed data Jack identified: staff maintaining status in Podio *alongside* the operational system (AppFolio during leasing, Buildertrend during construction). Two hundred owner-LLC mismatches are not 200 careless employees; they are the statistically inevitable output of hand-copying the same fact into multiple systems for years. **The root-cause fix is the single-entry future described in 05-data-sync-map.md — Helm becomes the middleware, Podio is retired — not a work queue of 335 manual corrections.** That said, a one-time bulk reconciliation (Helm-assisted, with the authoritative source pre-resolved per the playbooks above) is worth doing to establish a clean baseline before the sync work lands; a proposal for bulk-fix tooling belongs in the Helm backlog, not in staff to-do lists.
- **D17 + D18 = 60 compliance findings, and many of the expiries are old.** Observed expiry ages on the tour ranged from 2 days to 940 days (the oldest observed: lead-paint cert #748482 at 142 N Curley St, expired 2023-12-09), with many multi-hundred-day examples in between — the same stock-vs-flow backlog picture. This backlog predates Helm — the compliance page replaced a spreadsheet, and the spreadsheet evidently wasn't keeping up. Implication for the developer: these 60 are a *stock* problem (one-time remediation project) plus a *flow* problem (ongoing expiry alerting). Conflating them makes the 94.5%-vs-98% compliance score permanently ugly and trains users to ignore it. [confirm: should the pre-Helm compliance backlog be split out as a one-time remediation project with its own owner and deadline, separate from ongoing alerts?] (Per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11: D17/D18 render only on the separate Compliance page, not the Audit Findings page — `AuditPage.tsx:44,55` — so the two pages count different rule sets. The 94.5% score is computed client-side as `(total properties − distinct properties with a D17/D18 finding) / total properties`, but the denominator is the **sum of all phase counts including PH-X/PH-Z/UNKNOWN**, which can never trigger a license/cert rule — so the score is **structurally inflated** and can never reach a clean 100%. "Target 98%" is a hardcoded string, and the D18 card's "60 days" copy does not match the rule's 30-day threshold.)
- By contrast, **D1 (3), D7 (6), and D14 (19) are genuine workflow misses** — small counts, real operational meaning, worth individual human resolution. This is the population the eventual workflow-management layer (Jack's goal #2) should feed. **D5** and **D9** belong to this same low-count workflow-miss population when they fire (both returned zero rows at tour time, per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11).

## 4. Candidate new rules — PROPOSALS, not requirements

Everything in this section is a proposal for Jack and the developer to evaluate; most of it is not in the app. (Numbering note, per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11: the apparent D-gaps were never implemented — D2/D3/D4/D6/D8/D10/D12/D13/D15/D16/D19 have no code, ever — so any proposal below can safely claim a free D-number, and `10-common-data-map.md` reuses several of them. Note that two ideas below are now **partly built**: candidate #1 overlaps the shipped **D5**, and candidate #2 overlaps the shipped **D9** — see §2.)

**Proposed consistency rules (D-family):**

1. **Sold in one system, active in another.** Property is PH-X (sold/disposed) by one source but still has an active AppFolio owner/unit record, an open Podio Vacant Unit, or an open Buildertrend job. 87 properties are in PH-X today; stale residue in other systems inflates counts and risks phantom billing. *(Partly shipped as **D5** — Podio write-off/parked while AppFolio still active, per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11. The broader sold/disposed-vs-active-elsewhere check across all three systems is still a proposal.)*
2. **PH-5 (marketing/vacancy) with no AppFolio listing.** A vacant unit that isn't actually being marketed is pure carry-day leakage — arguably HIGH under the uniform day-cost model. *(Related check shipped as **D9** — unit vacant >14 days with no turnover activity, per Helm codebase read 2026-07-11. D9 keys on turnover records rather than the presence of an AppFolio listing, so the "vacant but unmarketed" variant here is still a distinct proposal.)*
3. **Buildertrend job complete but phase not advanced.** The construction-side mirror of D7: reno done in Buildertrend but Podio/Helm still shows PH-3, delaying the handoff to leasing.
4. **Closing complete but no settlement statement on file.** Victoria's ALTA/HUD-1s are what the Controller uses to establish cost basis in BNA; a PH-2 property without one blocks accounting downstream.
5. **In-service but missing BNA handoff.** Per company practice, once initial renovation completes, the property is turned over to DM and added to BNA depreciation software. A rule "property reached PH-4/PH-8 but no BNA in-service confirmation" would close that loop — but it requires an accounting-side signal Helm doesn't currently ingest. [confirm: does accounting want Helm to signal BNA/in-service events, and would they confirm in-service status back into Helm?]
6. **UNKNOWN phase as a first-class finding.** 7 properties currently have no derivable phase. If the phase can't be derived, at least one source is wrong or missing — that is itself a discrepancy.

**Proposed time-based rules (T-family):** distinct from D-rules by design. D-rules are point-in-time cross-system consistency checks; T-rules are duration violations — "property has been in PH-x for more than N days." Per Jack's cycle-time philosophy, every day costs the same, so T-rules should not rank phases against each other; they exist to *locate* where a property's carry days are accumulating and to trigger a human look. Clock definitions and threshold mechanics belong to 04-cycle-time-model.md; the audit engine would just be their enforcement surface.
